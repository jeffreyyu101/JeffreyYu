Designing a Universal Liquid 3D Printer

2017-2018 //Grades 11-12

This is my most recent research project focused around building a low-cost and efficient liquid 3D printer. Being a hobbyist, I was able to combine my hardware, software, and 3D printer-specific skills to work on an independent engineering research project. My printer utilized a unique three-part assembly: an open reservoir, peristaltic pump, and extruder assembly. I came up the project idea all the way back in sophomore year, was able to learn many of the skills needed during the summer of 2017 and began work during my junior year. I continued to work on it through the summer of 2018 and plan on submitting the project to the Regeneron Science Talent Search.

1st Place in Prototype Engineering, WAC Research Association Fair

Honors, Long Island Science Congress

The Effect of Groundwater Plumes on the Biodiversity of Long Island

2016-2018 //Grades 10-11

As part of the Barcode Long Island program, this project investigated how the infamous Northrop Grumman groundwater plumes in Bethpage, NY are affecting regional biodiversity. In collaboration with the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratories, my group was able to use DNA barcoding techniques to identify specimen samples collected from experimental and control sites. We found soil remediation efforts were successful in restoring soil biodiversity of contaminated sites. Being able study such an immediate community problem I hear pop up in local news again and again was very empowering, as well as taught me a lot about cutting-edge biotechnology techniques.

An Examination of the Effects of Music on Time Perception: Does Time Really Fly When You're Having Fun?

2016-2017 //Grade 10

Wandering into the world of behavioral science, sophomore year I conducted a study examining how music tempo and genre affects time perception. We conducted an experiment in classes at our school and found that those exposed to fast-pace music experience a duration of time longer than those exposed to slow-paced music, contradicting previous models of time perception. This study was a step out of my comfort zone branching into psychology, but I was captured by being able to study how the human brain works form a behavioral science standpoint.

Merit Award, Long Island Science Congress

Investigating the Effects of Sunscreen on the Environment

2015-2016 //Grade 9

My first high school research study investigated how sunscreen affects ocean water. Working with a team of three others, we collected gallons of seawater and studied the effects of how regular, water-resistant, waterproof, and biodegradable sunscreen had on the water quality. To our surprise, we discovered biodegradable sunscreen had the most detrimental effects. This study taught me a lot about the research process. I learned how to design and conduct a scientifically sound research experiment, run statistical tests, draw conclusions, and present our findings.
