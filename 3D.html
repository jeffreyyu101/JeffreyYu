Desk Light Electronics Box

This was a simple desk modification I made for my new working space. I installed LED light strips on the overhang of my desk shelves to illuminate the bench area. I wired everything myself, so I needed a project enclosure for the electronics and on/off switches. I decided to 3D design and print the box as shown here. The resulting product was a sleek black electronics control box that matches the aesthetic of my desk. I now use these lights every day, either for doing homework, working on other electronic projects, or just to brighten up my room.

Desk Coin Bank

This was another desk modification I made. My desk has two cupholders, but I don’t use them frequently and often becomes a place I store my spare change. I decided to make a custom coin bank that fits inside the hole for one of the cupholders. 3D designing and printing the coin bank, I now have a nice fitting coin bank that utilizes a space previously unused. The coin bank sits recessed inside the desk, so it is unobtrusive and now I wait for the day it reaches maximum capacity.

Wall Wire Grommet

After recent house renovations, I had to run wires through the walls to connect our landline, cable box, and computers to the modem and router. Drilling holes in the wall left an ugly finish, so I quickly drafted and printed a few of these wall wire grommets to make the holes look nice, as well to protect the cables from being damaged by the wall’s rough edges. This is an example of a quick and easy use of 3D printers in the home environment.
