JUULy the Bear

September 2018 //PennApps XVIII

Juuly the Bear is an application intended to fight the growth of vape culture by creating a counter culture that actively discourages Juuling while informing users of dangers. Juuly uses computer vision and machine learning algorithms combined with a social media scrapper to analyze Instagram profiles. If more than 40% of the images analyzed are of a person vaping, the user is classified as a “frequent e-cigarette” and sent a warning message as well as recovery resources via Facebook Messenger through a chat bot.

This was a glorious project to make, not only because of the humor, but also because I learned so much about computer vision and machine learning, topics I had no knowledge at all in before.

US Diplomatic Representation in Foreign Countries

Spring 2018 //MakeSPP

At MakeSPP, I was presented with The Correlates of War Diplomatic Exchange Data Set provided by the State Department. In my project, I used the D3.js JavaScript library to create a website that visualizes the levels of US diplomatic representation in foreign countries. The website depicts a world map and the levels of representation are portrayed as different colors within each country.

Projects like these are important for representing vast amounts of raw data that are not easily interpreted. Tech fluency can help government officials and the general public better comprehend such important information, and thereby making more informed and logical decisions. As someone interested in both government and technology, I hope to continue developing programs that will help the world understand important data representing foreign affairs and become a citizen diplomat myself.

Because of this project, I was invited to attend The United States Diplomacy Center and World Resources Institute’s first hackathon, ImpactHack. Read more about this project on the U.S Department of State's Offical Blog.

U.S. Department of State: Best Data Visualization using Diplomatic Exchange Data Set, MakeSPP

Silent Voice: an anti-bullying game

2017-2018 //Congressional App Challenge

Silent Voice is a choose your own adventure game with a goal to raise awareness about how bullying can affect mental health. The player makes various decisions throughout the game, choosing to be either an upstander or a bystander. In the end, the player is able to see the aftermath of his or her choices and how that affected the student being bullied.

Was selected as a winner for the Congressional App Challenge by Congressman Suozzi. Presented the app at Congress and the app is currently in display in the Cannon Tunnel.
